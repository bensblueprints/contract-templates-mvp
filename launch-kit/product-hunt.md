# Product Hunt launch — Contractly

**Name:** Contractly

**Tagline (60 chars):** 16 contract templates + fill-in-the-blanks editor. Pay once

**Description (260 chars):**
A local contract library for freelancers & small businesses: NDAs, contractor agreements, SOWs, releases + 12 more. {{variable}} form fills the blanks with live preview, rich-text clause editing, clean PDF export. $29 once — not $19/user/mo. Not legal advice.

**Full description:**
Contractly is a desktop contract workshop:

- 16 plain-language templates across 6 categories (NDAs, freelance/consulting, services, sales & rentals, real estate, misc)
- Placeholders like {{client_name}} become a form panel — type once, the document live-updates, a counter shows blanks remaining
- Edit any clause with rich text; insert your own variables; save the result as your reusable template
- Clone a finished contract for the next client — fields stay independent
- Export a clean, serif, Letter-format PDF; unfilled blanks export as ______ so nothing ships half-merged
- Injection-safe merging (field values can't become markup), 100% local JSON storage, no telemetry

Big honest disclaimer, in the app and in every template: these are starting points, not legal advice. For high-stakes work, pay the lawyer. For NDA #20 this month, stop paying monthly.

MIT source on GitHub. $29 gets the packaged Windows installer.

**Maker first comment:**
Hi PH 👋 Ben here. I got tired of paying subscription software for what is, at its core, a Word doc with blanks in it. Every freelance gig needs the same three documents — NDA, contractor agreement, sometimes an SOW — and the SaaS math for that is $228+/year forever.

Contractly is the blanks, the form that fills them, live preview, and the PDF. Plus the part I actually wanted: edit the clauses once, save it as YOUR template, clone per client.

Two honest limitations by design: (1) not legal advice — there's a permanent banner saying so, and I mean it; (2) no built-in e-signing — I already ship a pay-once e-signature tool (Inkseal), so Contractly exports HTML/PDF you can sign there. No reason to build it twice.

Source is MIT. Questions welcome!
